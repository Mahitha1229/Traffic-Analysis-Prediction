DL-02Why LSTM for Traffic?
The Temporal Problem
CONCEPT
Traffic is a sequence, not a table. 8AM volume depends on 7AM, 6AM, and yesterday's pattern. Random Forest treats each hour independently — it ignores sequential relationships.
LSTM's memory cells maintain a hidden state that carries information from previous timesteps — exactly what traffic prediction requires.
LSTM vs Random Forest
COMPARISON
RF (R²=0.912): Treats each hour independently. Good for feature importance. Cannot detect "traffic has been building for 3 hours".
LSTM (R²=0.929): Learns the buildup pattern. Knows 5AM→6AM→7AM means rush is coming. 1.7% better R² = ~570 fewer wrong predictions.
LSTM Gate Mechanism
THEORY
Forget Gate: Discards irrelevant old info (e.g., last week's holiday pattern).
Input Gate: Stores new relevant info (e.g., today is a weekday morning).
Output Gate: Produces prediction from current input + memory state.

DL-04Autoencoder Training & Findings
Why Autoencoder?
CONCEPT
Unsupervised Detection: No labelled anomaly data needed. The model learns "normal" and flags deviations automatically.
Real-world use: Accidents, road closures, festivals, and extreme weather cause unusual patterns the model cannot reconstruct — flagged as anomalies.
Autoencoder Training Loss
TRAINING
Key Findings
RESULTS
5% anomaly rate (876 records): 876 out of 17,520 hours showed unusual patterns not explained by normal seasonal or weather factors.
Most anomalies on: Holidays (Christmas, New Year), extreme weather, and Friday evenings during sports seasons showed highest reconstruction error.
43 critical anomalies (error > 2× threshold) were flagged as potential accidents or emergency events requiring investigation.

08Complete Findings & Conclusions
MapReduce Findings
HADOOP
Job 1 — Hourly: Two peaks at 06–09h (5,800 avg) and 15–18h (6,100 avg). Nights average 280 vehicles — 22× below peak hours.
Job 2 — Peak Detection: Friday 17:00 at 6,500 vehicles is the busiest slot of the week. Weekend peaks are 63% lower. Processed via HDFS streaming.
Hadoop vs Spark: MapReduce ~45s per job with disk I/O. Spark completes same task in ~4s using in-memory RDDs — 10× speedup demonstrated.
Deep Learning Findings
DL
LSTM beats RF by 6.7% RMSE: Temporal memory captures rush-hour buildup sequences that tabular models cannot detect.
Autoencoder detected 876 anomalies: 5% of records flagged. Correlated with holidays, extreme weather events, and suspected traffic incidents.
DNN (R²=0.921): Better than RF but worse than LSTM. Confirms that for time-series, sequence modelling is essential.
Dataset & Conclusions
SUMMARY
Dataset is sufficient: 17,520 hourly records × 2 years captures all seasons, days, weather types, and holidays — adequate for BDA + DL training.
Best approach: Hybrid pipeline — Spark for fast aggregation, LSTM for sequential prediction, Autoencoder for unsupervised anomaly detection.
Weather impact: Fog (−35%) and Snow (−28%) cause largest drops. LSTM handles these better as it has historical context of similar weather sequences.
